# 3D Extrema And Fold Network

Read with surface modules when peaks, troughs, basins, rims, saddles, cliffs, or
sharp folds define the skeleton.

Treat extrema as connected structures, not scalar decorations. Classify each
visible role: smooth ridge, fractured crease/rim, basin floor, cliff edge,
folded wall, lip, underside, or terminal peak. Preserve terminal character:
pointed, rounded, serrated, blunt, tapered, or truncated.

Build large-to-small:

1. envelope and projected footprint.
2. basin/rim or peak/ridge adjacency.
3. foreground, midground, rear, and side relief layers.
4. secondary folds and saddles tied to the primary structure.
5. local facets, material shading, and edge detail.

Do not fix an extremum by stretching the subject, breaking camera/aspect,
shrinking occupancy, or replacing a connected fold system with one isolated
Gaussian, cone, spike, pit, column, curtain, or slab. If this floor appears, the
next repair is fold-network construction, not color, material, or camera polish.
